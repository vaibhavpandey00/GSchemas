WATER




The Government of India has established National Water Mission as one of the eight National Missions under the National Action Plan on Climate Change.
The Union Cabinet approved (on 6th April, 2011) the comprehensive Mission Document for National Water Mission (NWM).
The main objective of NWM is “conservation of water, minimizing wastage and ensuring its more equitable distribution both across and within States through integrated water resources development and management”.

NWM has identified five goals as under:-

1. Comprehensive water data base in public domain and assessment of the impact of climate change on water resource,
2. Promotion of citizen and state actions for water conservation, augmentation and preservation,
3. Focused attention to vulnerable areas including over-exploited areas,
4. Increasing water use efficiency by 20%, and
5. Promotion of basin level integrated water resources management.
Various strategies for achieving the goals have been identified which lead to integrated planning for sustainable development with active participation of the stakeholders.
In pursuance to the approval accorded by the Union Cabinet to the National Water Mission, a Mission Directorate was established in the Ministry of Water Resources, River Development & Ganga Rejuvenation.
Eight Advisory Groups/Committees as envisaged in the NWM Mission document have been constituted.
“Implementation of National Water Mission (NWM)” Central Scheme of the Ministry of Water Resources with total financial outlay of Rs.196 crore (Rs. one hundred ninety six crore only) for the XII Plan period i.e upto March, 2017 has been approved by the Ministry of Finance vide MoWR office order No. 06/12/2012-NWM dated 27.11.2013.




The government is making active efforts to revolutionise villages and cities through block level water conservation schemes in the coming years.

For example; the Jal Gram Scheme under the Jal Kranti Abhiyan is aimed at developing two model villages in water starved areas to lead the other villages towards water conservation and preservation.



As of my last update in January 2022, there isn't a specific scheme called the "Bureau of Water Use Efficiency" in India. However, the Government of India has initiated various programs and schemes aimed at promoting water conservation, management, and efficiency across the country. One such initiative is the National Water Mission (NWM), which is one of the eight missions under the National Action Plan on Climate Change. The National Water Mission (NWM) was launched in 2011 with the objective of conserving water, minimizing wastage, and ensuring its efficient use. While the NWM doesn't specifically mention a "Bureau of Water Use Efficiency," it focuses on enhancing water use efficiency across various sectors, including agriculture, industry, urban areas, and rural communities. Here are some key components and objectives of the National Water Mission:

1. Promotion of Water Use Efficiency: The mission aims to promote the efficient use of water resources across different sectors by adopting technologies and practices that minimize wastage and maximize productivity.
2. Increasing Water Availability: It focuses on augmenting water supply through measures such as rainwater harvesting, groundwater recharge, and watershed management.
3. Enhancing Water Security: The mission aims to enhance water security by promoting sustainable water management practices and ensuring equitable access to water resources.
4. Awareness and Capacity Building: It emphasizes the importance of awareness and capacity building among stakeholders to encourage water conservation and efficient water use practices.
5. Data Collection and Monitoring: The NWM emphasizes the need for robust data collection, monitoring, and assessment of water resources to support evidence-based decision-making and policy formulation.
While there may not be a specific scheme named "Bureau of Water Use Efficiency," the objectives of various government initiatives, including the National Water Mission, align with the promotion of water use efficiency and conservation in India. The Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) is a flagship scheme launched by the Government of India aimed at enhancing irrigation efficiency, ensuring water availability, and improving agricultural productivity. It was launched in July 2015 with the vision of "Har Khet Ko Pani" (Water to Every Field). The scheme integrates various ongoing and new schemes related to water resources management, irrigation, and agricultural practices. Key components and objectives of the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) include:-

1. Har Khet Ko Pani: The primary objective of PMKSY is to ensure that every farm receives adequate water supply for irrigation purposes, thereby increasing agricultural productivity and reducing dependency on rain-fed agriculture.
2. Water Conservation: PMKSY emphasizes the importance of water conservation measures such as rainwater harvesting, construction of check dams, and groundwater recharge to optimize water usage and improve water availability for agriculture.
3. Micro-Irrigation: The scheme promotes the adoption of micro-irrigation techniques such as drip and sprinkler irrigation to enhance water use efficiency, minimize water wastage, and improve crop yield.
4. Watershed Development: PMKSY focuses on watershed management and development to conserve soil and water resources, prevent soil erosion, and promote sustainable agriculture practices.
5. Implementation: PMKSY is implemented through various components, including Accelerated Irrigation Benefit Programme (AIBP), Har Khet Ko Pani (Per Drop More Crop), Integrated Watershed Management Programme (IWMP), and On-Farm Water Management (OFWM), among others.
6. Funding: The scheme is funded through both central and state government contributions, with the central government providing financial assistance to states for the implementation of various components of PMKSY.
7. Capacity Building and Awareness: PMKSY focuses on capacity building, training, and awareness programs to educate farmers about modern irrigation techniques, water-saving practices, and sustainable agriculture methods.
Overall, the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) aims to transform Indian agriculture by ensuring efficient water use, improving irrigation infrastructure, and enhancing agricultural productivity, thereby contributing to rural development and farmers' welfare.




The Atal Bhujal Yojana (ABHY) is a scheme launched by the Government of India aimed at sustainable management of groundwater resources. It was launched in December 2019 with the objective of addressing the critical issue of groundwater depletion in select areas of the country. The scheme is named after former Prime Minister of India, Atal Bihari Vajpayee. Key features and objectives of the Atal Bhujal Yojana include:-

1. Groundwater Management: ABHY focuses on the sustainable management of groundwater resources in identified areas, particularly in stressed or over-exploited aquifers. The aim is to arrest the declining groundwater levels and promote sustainable use.
2. Community Participation: The scheme emphasizes the active involvement of local communities, including farmers, village councils, and water user associations, in groundwater management activities. Community participation is considered crucial for the success and sustainability of the interventions under ABHY.
3. Capacity Building: ABHY includes provisions for capacity building and training of stakeholders involved in groundwater management. This may include training programs for farmers on water-saving agricultural practices, awareness campaigns on groundwater conservation, and capacity building for local institutions involved in water management.
4. Demand-Side Management: The scheme promotes demand-side management measures to reduce the dependency on groundwater and encourage water conservation practices. This may include promoting the use of water-efficient irrigation techniques, rainwater harvesting, and crop diversification.
5. Technology Adoption: ABHY encourages the adoption of modern technology and tools for groundwater assessment, monitoring, and management. This may involve the use of remote sensing, GIS (Geographic Information System), and hydrogeological studies to better understand groundwater dynamics and plan interventions accordingly.
6. Incentives and Support: The scheme provides financial incentives and support to participating states for implementing groundwater management activities. This may include funding for infrastructure development, capacity building initiatives, and awareness programs.
Collaborative Approach: ABHY follows a collaborative approach involving coordination between central and state government agencies, local authorities, and other stakeholders. It aims to foster partnerships and cooperation to achieve sustainable groundwater management goals. Overall, the Atal Bhujal Yojana (ABHY) seeks to address the challenges of groundwater depletion through a combination of community participation, capacity building, demand-side management, and technological interventions. By promoting sustainable groundwater management practices, the scheme aims to ensure water security and resilience in the participating regions.

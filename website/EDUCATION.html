Duration

Scholarships are paid from Class IX to Class XII.

Amount

A total annual amount of Rs. 6000 is paid to 100,000 students across the country.

Eligibility

Students enrolled in a government-aided or local body run schools, belonging to families whose annual income is less than Rs.1,50,000 from all the sources combined, are eligible for receiving the scholarship. Students have to take up studies in approved courses in the government/aided/local body run schools. They have to maintain good conduct, as certified by the Head of the institution. They have to be full-time students. Leaves should be taken via proper manner and should not absent themselves otherwise. They should not be employed. Students getting clear promotion from Class VIII to Class IX are only eligible for receiving the scholarship.
Other criteria

• Each state and UT has fixed quota of scholarship, which are determined by the basis of population of children of age group of class VII and class VIII, and children enrolled in class VII and class VIII.
• Scholarships valid for studying courses in India for a period of four years maximum.
• No scholarships for pursuing studies outside India.
• No scholarships will be paid if the student leaves the course within one month of registration/admission.
• After the passage of 12 months of the academic year for which the claim has been made, no claims can be made for due arrears of the scholarship.
• No scholarships for diploma courses.
• Students from Kendriya Vidyalaya and Jawahar Vavodaya Vidyalaya are not eligible for the scholarship. Students studying in non-recognized schools will not be eligible for the scholarship as well.
• Candidates should clear the Class IX examination and Class XI examination with 55% marks, to be eligible to receive scholarships for Class X and Class XII. This is relaxed by 5 % for SC/ST candidates.
• Candidates should clear Class X examinations with 60% marks to eligible for continuing scholarship in class XI.
• If the student falls ill and is unable to attend the final examination, they should submit a medical certificate from a Registered Medical Practitioner to the Head of the Institution, certifying the duration of the illness. Student would be considered eligible for the scholarship if the attendance is over 50% for the year in aggregate.
• If a school does not have the examination at the end of class IX or Class XI, scholarship will be disbursed after submission of a written declaration by the Head of the Institution certifying the same.
NMMS Exam Fee

The Examination Fees is Rs. 100/- per student and affiliation fees of Rs. 200/- per year per institution.

Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA) is a centrally sponsored scheme launched by the Government of India in 2009. The primary objective of RMSA is to enhance access to secondary education and improve its quality across the country. It aims to provide opportunities for holistic development of students, particularly those residing in rural and remote areas. Key features and objectives of RMSA include:

1. Universal Access: Ensuring access to secondary education for all young people in the age group of 14-18 years by 2017, with a focus on disadvantaged groups.
2. Quality Improvement:Enhancing the quality of secondary education through various interventions such as improving infrastructure, providing teacher training, updating curriculum, and strengthening assessment systems.
3. Equity: Reducing disparities in access to secondary education by targeting underserved areas and marginalized groups.
4. Vocational Education: Integrating vocational education programs into the secondary school curriculum to provide students with skills relevant to the job market.
5. ICT Integration: Promoting the use of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) in secondary education to enhance teaching and learning processes.
6. Inclusive Education: Ensuring that education is inclusive and caters to the diverse needs of all learners, including those with disabilities.
7. Community Mobilization: Involving local communities, parents, and other stakeholders in the planning and implementation of secondary education programs.
RMSA works in collaboration with state governments to implement its objectives effectively. It provides financial assistance to states and Union Territories for various initiatives aimed at improving secondary education. The scheme has played a significant role in expanding access to secondary education and improving its quality, particularly in rural and remote areas of India.

The Mid-Day Meal Scheme (MDMS) is one of the most significant interventions in the realm of education and nutrition in India. Here are its key features:

1. Free Meals: The scheme provides free, nutritious meals to children in government and government-aided schools, including those run under Education Guarantee Scheme (EGS) and Alternative & Innovative Education (AIE) centers.
2. Universal Coverage: It covers children from primary to upper primary classes, typically aged between 6 and 14 years, in both urban and rural areas.
3. Nutritional Standards: The meals provided under the scheme are designed to meet the nutritional standards laid down by the government, ensuring that children receive adequate nourishment for their physical and cognitive development.
4. Encourages Attendance: By providing free meals, the scheme aims to increase school attendance and retention rates, particularly among children from socio-economically disadvantaged backgrounds.
5. Local Community Involvement: The scheme encourages the involvement of local communities, such as self-help groups, parents, and voluntary organizations, in the preparation and serving of meals. This not only helps in efficient implementation but also fosters community participation in education.
6. Focus on Quality and Hygiene: There is an emphasis on maintaining quality and hygiene standards in the preparation, storage, and serving of meals to ensure the safety and well-being of the children.
7. Provision of Infrastructure: In many cases, the scheme includes provisions for the construction of kitchen-cum-stores, procurement of cooking devices, and other infrastructure required for meal preparation.
8. Supplementary Nutrition: In addition to cooked meals, the scheme also provides supplementary nutrition in the form of fruits, eggs, milk, etc., to enhance the nutritional value of the meals.
9. Mid-Day Meal Monitoring: Monitoring mechanisms are put in place to ensure the effective implementation of the scheme, including regular inspections, monitoring committees, and grievance redressal mechanisms.
Overall, the Mid-Day Meal Scheme plays a crucial role in addressing malnutrition, enhancing school attendance, and promoting the overall well-being of children, especially those belonging to marginalized communities.
